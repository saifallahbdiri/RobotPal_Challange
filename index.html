Zaghoun is a picturesque town located in northeastern Tunisia, known for its natural beauty and historical charm. Nestled at the foot of the Atlas Mountains, it offers stunning views and a tranquil atmosphere. The town is famous for its ancient Roman water temple and the source of the aqueduct that once supplied Carthage. Zaghoun is also known for its traditional crafts, especially pottery and handmade goods. Surrounded by lush olive groves and orchards, it is a hub for agriculture and local produce. Its blend of history, nature, and culture makes Zaghoun a hidden gem worth visiting.
